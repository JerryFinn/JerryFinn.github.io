Structure of the Presentation

This will be an interactive slideshow. The user will be guided through 3 scenes. They will be able to mouseover certain data points to reveal annotations about the specific data (where appropriate) that will help them drill down. On each scene the user can choose a parameter from a drop down selection that will trigger a more refined view of the data.

Introduction

The question being asked, "Is there a correlation between unemployment and disability?" was asked by leading labor economist David Autor in his influential 2011 paper "The Unsustainable Rise of the Disability Rolls in the United States". He found a correlation from the mid-1980s to 2009. Our question is, "Does this correlation hold from 2009 to 2015?" We get data from the county level and look at the national and state trends. Professor Autor's study ended right before the Great Recession where unemployment spiked rapidly and then steadily declined. This gives a unique period to study unlike any interval studied in Professor Autor's paper.
